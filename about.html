Other Experience

One of the overarching themes of my career is that I want to achieve competency in as many aspects of library & info science as I am naturally predisposed to achieving mastery of, and then to go from there.

 

To these ends-- 

 I have assisted with Every Child Ready to Read, as well as developing system-wide materials for the program, and have been a coordinator for Summer Reading Program. 

 

I have experience forming and maintaining a Teen Library Council, and working with various community organizations, in particular, Friends of Joe Brown Park. 

 

I am familiar with general Security issues in a library, created and maintained the Security log at East New Orleans Regional Library, and have studied Warren Graham's Blackbelt Librarian, in addition to seeing him speak. 

 

I am fairly social-media savvy, and am one of the administrators of the New Orleans Public Library's central Facebook page. I was also the administrator of the East New Orleans Regional Library's Facebook page, one of the most successful in terms of reach and engagement, in the system.

 

Additionally, I have assisted in the management of a Regional Library through its opening (storing, moving, unpacking, shifting), and dealt with an ILS migration (from Horizon to Polaris). This process was assisted by my graduate study of Change Management.

 

I have been CPR certified (expired October 2013).

 

I have been trained to mend items, and am somewhat experienced at doing so.

-- Comedian and author Paula Poundstone, national spokesperson for Friends of Libraries U.S.A. (FOLUSA)
